# IPPM and BMWG Joint WG Charter

The xxxx Working Group develops and maintains standard metrics, methodologies and protocols that can be applied to the quality, performance, and reliability of Internet data delivery services and applications running over public and private networks using IETF technologies.

The methodologies cover management, control, and forwarding planes and they apply to hardware, virtualized, and containerized network infrastructure, including endpoints, in production and lab environments. Where in lab environments the testing procedures ensure consistent, reproducible and reportable benchmarking results.

These metrics, protocols, and methodologies are designed as such that they can be used by network operators, end users, or independent testing groups in production or lab environments. Metrics developed are intended to provide unbiased quantitative performance measurements.

The xxxx WG fosters commonality and comparability of metrics and measurements across IETF protocols at different layers. It closely collaborates with the Performance Measurement Directorate and gives with RFC 6390 guidelines on new performance metric developments and with RFC 7799 terminology for authoring.


## Work Items

It works on evolving active, passive and hybrid measurement protocols such as TWAMP, STAMP, IOAM, Alternate Marking and Hybrid Two-Step (HTS) and the definition of its YANG models for configuration, operation and Network Telemetry data export.

Its work is limited to metrics and methodologies which are applicable to the IP and MPLS data plane and its upper transport-layer protocols such as TCP, UDP and QUIC. Other aspects, such as security and layer 2, can be covered as well but requires explicit approval from the area director. This working group does not specify encapsulations required for measurements over non-IP and non-MPLS layers. 


## Relationship With Existing WGs

It dispatches and coordinates performance measurement related work with other IETF working groups such as MPLS, 6MAN and SPRING where encapsulations are specified.

It liaisons with other standards development organization such as ITU-U, IEEE, 3GPP and BBF and reaches out to other operator communities such as NANOG, RIPE, and APRICOT.
