# IPPM and BMWG Joint WG Charter

The xxxx Working Group develops and maintains metrics, methodologies, and protocols that can be applied to the quality, performance, and reliability of Internet data delivery services and applications running over public and private networks using IETF technologies.

The methodologies cover management, control, and forwarding planes and they apply to hardware, virtualized, and containerized network infrastructure (including endpoints) in production and lab environments. Where in lab environments the testing procedures ensure consistent, reproducible, and reportable benchmarking results.

These metrics, protocols, and methodologies are designed as such that they can be used by network operators, end users, or independent testing groups in production or lab environments. Metrics developed are intended to provide unbiased quantitative performance measurements.

The xxxx WG fosters commonality and comparability of metrics and measurements across IETF protocols at different layers. The WG closely collaborates with the Performance Measurement Directorate and gives with RFC 6390 guidelines on new performance metric developments and with RFC 7799 terminology for authoring.

## Work Items

The WG works on evolving active, passive, and hybrid measurement protocols (e.g., TWAMP, STAMP, IOAM, Alternate Marking and Hybrid Two-Step (HTS)) and the definition of its YANG data models for configuration, operation, and Network Telemetry data export.

Its work is limited to metrics and methodologies which are applicable to the IP and MPLS data plane and its upper transport-layer protocols (e.g., TCP, UDP, and QUIC). Other aspects, such as security and Layer 2, can be covered as well but requires explicit approval from the area director. This WG does not specify encapsulations required for measurements over non-IP and non-MPLS layers.

The WG is responsible for the maintenance of RFCs published by BMWG and IPPM WGs.

These metrics and protocols can be published as Standards Track or Experimental while methodologies can be published as Informational or BCP. Also, the WG documents Best Current Practices for operations of the performance-related protocols and methodologies.

## Relationship With Existing WGs

The WG is resposnible for coordinating and dispatching performance measurement related work within the IETF. It specifically coordinate with other WGs such as MPLS, 6MAN, and SPRING where encapsulations are specified.

The WG liaisons with other Standards Development Organizations such as ITU-U, IEEE, 3GPP, and BBF and reaches out to other operator communities such as NANOG, RIPE, and APRICOT.
